Cost of Living in Major Canadian Cities for Foreign Workers

1. Toronto

Toronto is Canada's largest city, and it comes with a high cost of living. Rent for a one-bedroom apartment in the downtown area can exceed CAD 2,500 per month.

2. Vancouver

Vancouver is another expensive city, with a one-bedroom apartment rental averaging CAD 2,200 in the downtown area. The city is known for its natural beauty and high-quality living standards.

3. Montreal

Montreal offers more affordable living options compared to Toronto and Vancouver, with one-bedroom apartments available for CAD 1,500 per month or less. The city also boasts a rich cultural scene.

4. Ottawa

Ottawa, the capital city, offers a more moderate cost of living. Rent for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center typically ranges from CAD 1,500 to CAD 1,800 per month.

5. Calgary

Calgary offers a lower cost of living, with rental prices averaging CAD 1,400 for a one-bedroom apartment. The city has a strong economy driven by the oil and gas industry.

Conclusion

The cost of living in Canada varies depending on the city you choose. While major cities like Toronto and Vancouver are expensive, other cities like Montreal and Ottawa offer more affordable options for foreign workers.
