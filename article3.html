How to Get a Job in Canada Without a Work Permit

1. Volunteer Work

Volunteering is a great way to gain experience and get your foot in the door of Canadian companies, though you must make sure it is truly voluntary and not considered a paid role.

2. Working on a Study Permit

If you're studying in Canada, you may be eligible to work on or off-campus while enrolled in a designated learning institution. Check your study permit conditions to ensure you meet the eligibility criteria.

4. Job Offer and LMIA

If you receive a job offer, the employer must apply for a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) to prove that there is no Canadian worker available for the job. Once approved, you can apply for a work permit.

Conclusion

While it's challenging to get a job in Canada without a work permit, it is possible through alternatives like volunteering, studying, or networking. Be sure to follow the correct immigration steps once you secure an offer.
